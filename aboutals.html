About Alternative Learning System - ALS

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is a parallel learning system in the Philippines designed to provide a practical and flexible option for out-of-school children, youth, and adults who are unable to access or complete formal education.

üìö Overview of ALS

Mandated by: The Department of Education (DepEd)

Target Learners:

Out-of-school youth and adults (OSYA) who have dropped out of formal education or have never had access to it.

üéØ Goals of ALS:

Literacy and numeracy skills. Lifelong learning opportunities. Equivalency programs (Elementary and Junior High School level). Skills and livelihood training. Reintegration into formal education or employment

üìù ALS Assessment and Certification:

Learners take the ALS A\&E Test, administered by DepEd. Passers receive a certificate equivalent to a formal school diploma. They can proceed to Senior High School or employment.

K-12 Curriculum Alignment:

ALS uses a contextualized curriculum that is aligned with the K-12 Basic Education Curriculum of the formal school system, but it is not a mirror image of it.

üåü Benefits of ALS:

Offers second chances at education; Empowers marginalized groups; Promotes inclusive and equitable learning; Prepares learners for employment, entrepreneurship, or further education
